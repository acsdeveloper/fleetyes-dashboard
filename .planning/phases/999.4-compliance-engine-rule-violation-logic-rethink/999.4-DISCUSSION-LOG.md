## Area: Violation Date Attribution

The bug is that aggregate-window rules emit a "window start" date instead of the date
of the working day where the limit was crossed.

| Rule | Current `date` field | Should be |
|------|----------------------|-----------|
| Weekly driving | `ws` (Monday of week) | Last day of week that crossed 56h |
| Fortnightly driving | `ws` (Monday of fortnight) | Last day that crossed 90h |
| Weekly rest | `ws` (Monday of week) | First working day of rest-deficient week |
| Daily rest | `curr.date` ✓ | No change needed |
| Consecutive days | `day.date` ✓ | No change needed |

**Decision:** Fix all three aggregate rules. Do NOT change `getCellCompliance` — it's correct.

---

## Area: Trip Context

**Decision:** Add `tripId?`, `tripStartTime?`, `tripEndTime?` to `ComplianceViolation`.
Add `orderId?: string` to `Activity` so adapter can tag activities with their source Order UUID.
Prospective check violations get full trip context (the new trip IS the cause).
Aggregate violations leave `tripId` undefined (multi-trip, no single cause).

## Area: Diagnostic Noise
